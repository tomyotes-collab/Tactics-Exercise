Key Concepts — How Tactics Work

1 · What tactics are

Tactics are the actions undertaken to achieve the goals and SMART objectives of a social media strategy. They are also the vehicles that deliver the (sub) key messages to the target audience (Sutherland, 2024). The order matters: goal → SMART objectives → key messages → then tactics. Each tactic should be built from a sub-key message — never the other way around.

2 · Blending online and offline: the propinquital loop

Offline events are powerful tactics: they deepen relationships and create long-lasting memories between people and a brand. Online tactics can spark user-generated content (UGC).

A propinquital loop blends the two: using social media as a bridge to strengthen relationships with current and prospective customers in between face-to-face interactions (Sutherland et al., 2018).

3 · Why integrate the two?

  • Synergy and reach — offline events create memorable, face-to-face experiences; social media extends them to wider audiences and over longer timeframes.
  • Brand outcomes — integrated campaigns improve brand image, community orientation and loyalty (Campillo-Alhama & Martínez-Sala, 2019; Huang & Wu, 2024; Kim et al., 2016).
  • Classic example — Red Bull "Stratos": a spectacular offline event amplified globally through social media.
